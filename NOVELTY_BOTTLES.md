# Novelty Bottle Line — Research & Plan

Deep-dive on the "faux apothecary" + "motivational sarcasm beverage" angles, supporting UV_PRINT_PRODUCTS.md §4f spotlight. Compiled 2026-04-18.

---

## 1. Concept Summary

Two sibling product lines sharing the same printer workflow, suppliers, and warehouse:

**Line A — Faux Apothecary** (vintage / witchcore / Halloween / speakeasy)
- Amber or clear apothecary-style bottles with cork stoppers
- Fake "tonic / elixir / poison / remedy" branding
- Original fake brands, faux-vintage aesthetic (pre-1920s inspired — inherently public-domain look)
- Target: goth/witchcore year-round, October spike, home bar / speakeasy decor, prop/cosplay

**Line B — Motivational Sarcasm Beverages** (Prank-O / Omega Mart aesthetic)
- Beer-style, soda-style, or small cola-style bottles
- Fake brands: "Liquid Patience", "Give-a-Damn Serum", "Adulting Antidote", "Courage in a Bottle"
- Target: gag gift buyers, bachelorette/birthday, office humor, novelty-shop retail

**Shared workflow:** empty glass bottles → UV-print direct on glass (cylindrical rotary jig) → cork or crown cap → giftable packaging. No food-safe complexity if sold **decorative only**.

---

## 2. Strategic Decision: Decorative vs. Actual Beverage

Three paths, in order of risk/cost:

### Path 1 — Decorative only (RECOMMENDED START)
- Sell **empty bottles** as collectibles / decor / gag gifts
- Label: "Novelty decorative item. Not for consumption."
- No FDA compliance, no nutrition facts, no co-packer
- Gross margin ~75–85%, turnaround: days
- Precedent: Prank-O sells **empty gift boxes**, not real products — and built a multi-million-dollar business

### Path 2 — Add optional filler (SECONDARY PHASE)
- Jelly beans, small candies, bath salts, dried lavender, tea bags
- "Fillable" framing — buyer adds their own mixer/shot
- Still skirts FDA beverage rules because the product is a *container*, not a beverage
- Adds 20–40% to price, 30–60% to perceived value

### Path 3 — Actual private-label beverage (LONG-TERM ONLY)
- Work with a beverage co-packer to produce real soda/tonic
- **Bottle MOQ: 500–5,000 units** (lower than canned 2,000–5,000 cases)
- Timeline: **8–12 weeks** formula → first production
- Requires: full FDA nutrition label, statement of identity, distributor info, lab analysis, liability insurance
- Capital: typically $10,000–$50,000+ for first run
- Skip this until Path 1 has validated demand for 6–12 months

**My recommendation:** launch Path 1 only. Validate the aesthetic sells before touching any FDA-regulated path.

## 4. Private-Label Actual Soda Angle (Path 3 deep-dive)

For reference — don't start here, but worth knowing the shape of it.

**What rebranding actually means legally:**
- You **cannot** buy Kroger/Sam's Club/any generic-brand soda and put your label on it (trademark infringement + FDA mislabeling)
- You **can** contract a co-packer to produce *unbranded* soda under your label (private label / white label) — this is legitimate rebranding
- You are then the "distributor of record" and carry full FDA labeling liability

**Reality of co-packer economics:**
- Glass/PET bottling MOQ: **500–5,000 units** per SKU
- Canned beverage MOQ: **50,000+ units** — skip
- Timeline: **8–12 weeks** formula approval → first run
- Reorders: **2–4 weeks** once materials are stocked
- Hidden fees to watch: inbound receiving, storage, rework, rush, freight surcharges
- Typical all-in cost: **$1.50–$4.00/unit** at low volume, dropping with scale

**FDA label must include:**
1. Statement of identity (e.g., "Carbonated Beverage")
2. Net contents
3. Manufacturer / packer / distributor name + address
4. Full nutrition facts panel
5. Ingredient list in descending order
6. No misleading claims

**Decision rule:** only pursue Path 3 if a single decorative SKU has cleared 500+ units sold in 90 days. Demand validation first.

## 5. FDA / Legal Guardrails (critical)

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Label decorative bottles "Not for consumption" | Imply any medical effect, real or joking |
| Use original fake brand names | Parody real brands (no fake Coca-Cola, fake Xanax, etc.) |
| Use pre-1920s faux-vintage aesthetic | Mimic real Rx label layouts (FDA + liability risk) |
| Sell empty or with non-consumable filler | Package with candy that looks like pills (CPSC concern) |
| Keep images and copy obviously satirical | Use health claims even in jest |
| Register a simple LLC before selling | Sell into CA without Prop 65 warnings on applicable products |

Sensitive words that raise regulatory flags (avoid on labels): *cures, treats, prevents, Rx, prescription, FDA approved, pharmaceutical, medicine, drug*.

Safe humor vocabulary: *tonic, elixir, remedy (antiquated), brew, potion, serum, antidote, bitters, compound, infusion.*

## 7. Go-to-Market Plan (first 90 days)

| Week | Actions |
|---|---|
| 1 | Order bottle samples: 12× 8oz amber (Line A), 12× 12oz beer bottles (Line B), cork stoppers, adhesion primer. Test UV print adhesion and cure on curved glass. |
| 2 | Buy/build rotary cylindrical jig for E1 (check 3djigsolutions). Design 3 Line A + 3 Line B SKUs in Illustrator/Affinity. |
| 3 | Register umbrella domain + LLC (most states ~$50–$200). Open Etsy shop. Shoot moody product photography (the aesthetic sells). |
| 4–5 | Print 50 units across 6 SKUs. List on Etsy. Seed Reddit (r/somethingimade, r/halloween, r/witchesvspatriarchy) with ethical posting. |
| 6–8 | Test Etsy ads at $5–$10/day. Measure conversion per SKU. Kill losers. |
| 9–10 | Reorder top 2–3 SKUs at 100+ units each. Approach AREA15 / Spirit Halloween / local oddities shops for wholesale. |
| 11–12 | Apply to Amazon Handmade (easier approval than Amazon Custom). Add gift-set bundles (3-packs, 6-packs). |

**Kill criteria:** if total revenue < $500 after 60 days across all SKUs, the product mix is wrong — redesign before reordering. If revenue > $2,000, scale the top SKU before adding new ones.
